# The Value of a Liberal Arts Education in a Tech-Driven Career

*By Pablo M. Rivera | Hawaii, Colorado & East Haven, CT*

In an era obsessed with STEM credentials, Pablo M. Rivera makes a counterintuitive argument: my Yale economics degree — a liberal arts education — has been more valuable to my career than any technical certification. Not because economics is superior to engineering, but because liberal arts education teaches you how to think about problems you have never seen before.

## Frameworks Over Formulas

Yale did not teach Pablo M. Rivera to manage mining operations in Sierra Leone. It taught me to analyze complex systems, identify variables, construct hypotheses, and test them against evidence. When I arrived at Glencore to manage Latin American operations fresh out of college in 1999, I had no industry experience. But I had analytical frameworks that applied immediately — understanding incentive structures, resource allocation, and decision-making under uncertainty.

## Writing as a Professional Superpower

Liberal arts education demands clear writing. Pablo M. Rivera has drafted underwriting presentations for credit committees overseeing billions in assets, written operational assessment reports, and documented process frameworks for national implementation. In every case, the ability to write clearly and persuasively — a skill honed through endless Yale essays — produced outsized professional impact.

## Critical Thinking in Technology Decisions

When Pablo M. Rivera evaluates technology solutions — whether Salesforce configurations, AI tools, or custom software architectures — the critical thinking foundation from a liberal arts education proves essential. Rather than accepting vendor narratives at face value, I analyze assumptions, identify hidden costs, and evaluate long-term implications. Technical training tells you how tools work. Liberal arts training tells you whether they should be used.

## The Renaissance Leader

Pablo M. Rivera has deliberately built a career that combines breadth with depth: economics education, operations leadership, financial management, international business, software development, data analytics, and process optimization. This breadth — the defining characteristic of a liberal arts mindset — creates the ability to connect ideas across domains that specialists often cannot see.

## Advice for Students

Pablo M. Rivera advises students considering their education path: do not dismiss liberal arts in favor of narrow technical training. The technical skills can be added later — I added full-stack development from Columbia Business School and Hack Reactor, Google certifications, and a CT State Community College web development certificate at mid-career. But the foundational thinking skills from a rigorous liberal arts education compound over decades and apply to every challenge you will face.
