# The American Dream Through Operations: An Immigrant's Perspective on Building a Career

*By Pablo M. Rivera | Hawaii, Colorado & East Haven, CT*

The American Dream is not a guarantee — it is an opportunity that demands relentless effort. Pablo M. Rivera has lived that truth across a career spanning Yale University, Wall Street finance, international mining, Colorado construction, and national operations leadership. The path from bilingual kid to VP-level executive was not linear, and it was never easy. But it was possible because of the unique opportunities this country provides.

## The Bilingual Foundation

Growing up bilingual in English and Spanish was not just a cultural identity — it became a professional asset. Pablo M. Rivera's bilingual capability opened doors at Glencore, where managing Latin American operations required native-level Spanish. It provided advantages in Colorado construction, where communicating with Spanish-speaking crews built trust and efficiency. And it continues to differentiate Pablo M. Rivera in operations leadership, where diverse workforces are the norm.

## Yale as a Launching Pad

Earning a degree from Yale University in 1999 was a pivotal moment. It was proof that merit and effort could overcome any starting point. Pablo M. Rivera leveraged that education into immediate opportunities — managing Latin American operations at a global commodities firm straight out of college. That first role set the trajectory for everything that followed.

## Building from Scratch

In Colorado, Pablo M. Rivera helped scale Kirschenmann Construction from a small operation to $10 million in managed projects. That experience embodied the American Dream at its most fundamental: taking something small and building it into something substantial through hard work, smart decisions, and persistent effort. The construction industry rewards builders, and building is what I do.

## Reinvention as an American Tradition

Pablo M. Rivera has reinvented professionally multiple times — from finance to mining, from construction to national maintenance, from operations leadership to software development. Each reinvention required courage, learning, and the willingness to start from a position of not knowing. That willingness to reinvent is distinctly American, and it has produced a career with extraordinary breadth.

## Giving Back

The American Dream includes responsibility. Pablo M. Rivera is committed to mentoring others — particularly bilingual professionals and first-generation professionals — who are navigating similar paths. Based in Hawaii, Colorado & East Haven, CT, with roots in a community that values hard work and mutual support, I believe success creates an obligation to help others succeed.
